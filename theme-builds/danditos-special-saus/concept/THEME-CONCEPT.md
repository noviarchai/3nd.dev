# Theme Concept: Dandito's Special Saus

## Quick Summary
A premium, bold, hand-crafted WordPress theme for Dandito's Special Saus — a Calgary-based artisanal hot sauce brand with a rebellious "NO FACTORY SAUCE. NO SHORTCUTS." attitude. The theme captures the fiery passion of craft food production with warm, spicy visual language, immersive product storytelling, and a WooCommerce-powered store that feels as premium as the sauce inside every bottle.

## Target Audience
**Primary:** Hot sauce enthusiasts, craft food lovers, Calgary locals aged 25-55 who value quality ingredients and local sourcing.
**Behavior:** Shops on mobile during commutes, researches products deeply before buying, shares finds on social media, seeks products with authentic stories.
**Frustrations:** Generic food labels, mass-produced sauces with vague "artisan" marketing, sites that feel corporate rather than handcrafted.

## Brand Personality
- **Bold** — unapologetic heat, strong flavors, confident voice
- **Artisanal** — hand-crafted, small-batch, pepper-by-pepper care
- **Rebellious** — anti-factory, anti-shortcuts, anti-corporate food
- **Authentic** — real story, real ingredients, real Calgary roots
- **Warm** — fire brings people together, not just heat

## Visual Direction
Inspired by: High-end craft hot sauce labels (Bravado, Melange, Torchkeeper), craft beer packaging aesthetics, artisanal food magazines like Cherry Bombe and Kinfolk FOOD.
- Dark charcoal/oil backgrounds as the canvas — like the dark of a well-seasoned cast iron
- Fire reds, ember oranges, and chili pepper accents against near-black surfaces
- Typography: bold editorial display faces mixed with confident sans-serif body — like a food magazine cover meets a craft brewery
- Generous product photography spaces — the bottle is the hero
- Subtle flame/heat motion textures, ember glow effects
- Hand-drawn or stamped elements for authenticity markers
- Film-grain warmth on hero sections

## Feature Priorities
1. MUST HAVE: Hero-driven homepage with full-bleed imagery and brand story
2. MUST HAVE: WooCommerce shop with premium product pages, gallery zoom, related products
3. MUST HAVE: About/Story page with brand narrative and craft process
4. MUST HAVE: Where to Buy / Find Us page (retail locations)
5. MUST HAVE: Contact page with form
6. SHOULD HAVE: Mini-cart with slide-out panel, quick add-to-cart
7. SHOULD HAVE: Newsletter signup in footer with warmth/personal voice
8. NICE TO HAVE: Product ingredient transparency section
9. NICE TO HAVE: Heat level indicator system on products

## Differentiation Statement
Unlike generic food themes that treat products as inventory items, Dandito's Special Saus theme treats every bottle as a story waiting to be told. The shopping experience feels like discovering a hidden gem at a farmers market — warm, personal, and unmistakably crafted with intention.

## Competitive References
1. Bravado Spice Co. website — bold typography, dark palette, premium feel
2. High River Brewing (craft brewery aesthetic) — Calgary local, artisanal, authentic
3. Cherry Bombe magazine editorial style — warm food photography, confident layouts

## Color Palette Direction (mood notes — hex codes in Visual Design System)
- **Primary mood:** Dark, smoky, like the inside of a smokehouse — near-black charcoal backgrounds
- **Secondary mood:** Warm ember glow — deep reds fading to orange
- **Accent mood:** Chili pepper highlight — bright red-orange for CTAs and badges
- **Text mood:** Warm white on dark (not cold #fff), warm cream

## Typography Direction (mood notes — specifics in Visual Design System)
- **Display/Headlines:** Bold, impactful, slightly condensed — commands attention like a hot sauce label
- **Body:** Warm, readable, friendly — not clinical or corporate
- **Accent/labels:** Hand-stamped or badge feel for heat levels and badges

## Layout Rhythm
- Hero sections: full-bleed, immersive, minimal text overlay
- Product sections: breathing room around bottles, photography-first
- Story sections: editorial magazine-style — text and image in dynamic conversation
- Dense-but-breathing: enough content to feel abundant, white space to feel premium
- Asymmetric grid for blog/news sections — editorial, not templated

## Page Types Required
1. Homepage — hero story, featured products, brand pillars, newsletter
2. Shop (WooCommerce archive) — grid of products with heat-level badges
3. Single Product — gallery with zoom, story, ingredients, related products
4. About/Story — narrative brand page with founder story, craft process
5. Where to Buy — retail location list/map
6. Contact — contact form with personality
7. Cart — slide-out mini-cart + full cart page
8. Checkout — branded, clean, trustworthy
9. My Account — for returning customers

## WooCommerce Requirements
- Product gallery with zoom on hover
- Heat level badge system (mild/medium/hot/extremé)
- Featured/new product badges
- Related products on single product page
- Mini-cart with slide-out panel
- Full cart page with product imagery
- Branded checkout experience
- Account dashboard for returning customers
- Sale price display with original struck through

## Interaction Style
- Fire-warm micro-animations: subtle flame flicker on badges, ember glow on hover
- Smooth scroll with section reveals — content feels like it's rising from heat
- Product cards lift and glow subtly on hover — like picking up a bottle from a shelf
- Navigation: bold, clear, fire-red active states
- CTA buttons: bold, rounded, glow effect on hover
- Mobile: swipe-friendly product gallery, sticky add-to-cart bar
